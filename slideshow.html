Rainwater is one of the most important natural resources for the Waorani people, yet it is variable and unpredicatable, so each drop is coveted.

As communities grow, their need for potable water increases, and the dry months compound scarcity even more.

The melding of cultures--ancient and modern, indigenous and foreign--is leading to a noticeable loss of ancestral knowledge, native language, and generational separation.

The Waorani culture is transforming. Some might say it is disappearing. This reality deserves attention on behalf of the next generation.
